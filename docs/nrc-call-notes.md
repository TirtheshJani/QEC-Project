# NRC Applied Quantum Computing Challenge — call notes

Source: NRC's *Emerging directions in fault-tolerant quantum computing —
Call for proposals*, accessed May 2026. The 2025 cycle ran 2 June 2025 →
29 August 2025. We are planning for the 2026 cycle on the same calendar.

## What they fund

> Up to $4.2M total, anticipated up to $400k per project as non-repayable
> grants/contributions, over 2 years.

Eligible Canadian applicants: academic + research institutions, not-for-profit
research orgs, Canadian SMEs (<500 employees). International collaborators
may be eligible if the participation benefits Canada. **Project teams must
include at least one NRC researcher.**

## Three priority areas

1. Quantum algorithms for differential equations.
2. **QEC and fault tolerance.** ← our target.
3. NP approximation, combinatorics, and quantum computing.

## QEC priority area — sub-objectives (verbatim)

- Novel QEC codes: innovative coding architectures, better error resilience,
  reduced qubit overhead.
- Efficient fault-tolerant logical operations: protocols for FT logical
  ops within existing or newly designed QEC codes.
- **Decoding algorithm optimization:** high-efficiency, noise-resistant
  decoders capable of real-time signal recovery in noisy environments.
- Fault-tolerant quantum computing architectures: integrated frameworks
  combining QEC with quantum algorithms, minimal overhead.
- Fundamental exploration of QEC: unified theoretical frameworks; bounds.
- Interdisciplinary innovations: info theory, statistical physics, graph
  theory applied to QEC.

Our capstone targets the **third** bullet.

## EOI evaluation criteria (annex A — 7 criteria, equally weighted)

1. **Methodology.** Well-developed workplan, key tasks, viable methodology.
2. **Project team and resources.** Roles, ability, capacity over project
   lifetime; EDI considered.
3. **Alignment with scope.** Clear statement of how it addresses the call
   objectives.
4. **Addressing a gap.** Value of technology + knowledge gap; risks if
   not funded.
5. **Innovation.** Novelty vs Canadian and international work; metrics of
   success (IP, publications, HQP training, etc.).
6. **Economic and social impacts.** Quantitative estimate of benefits.
7. **NRC collaboration.** NRC lead researcher identified; leverage of NRC
   resources; feasibility against existing NRC capacity.
